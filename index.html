Welcome to Anthropology of Disability

A comprehensive exploration of disability in the social sciences.

This course examines the genealogy of disability as a topic in anthropology and related disciplines. Through the duration of this course, students will engage with key texts, contemporary ethnographies, and participate in events that highlight the intersection of disability with other forms of activism.

Weekly Content

Each week covers different aspects of the disability studies, exploring theories, key works, and modern applications in activism.

Discover projects from students as they explore the intricate connections between disability and societal constructs.
